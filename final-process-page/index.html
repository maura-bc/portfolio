User Interface Principles

Von Restorff Effect

When similar items are grouped together, the item that looks different is more likely to stand out and be remembered.

I used this in the benefits grid by highlighting the Earn on Rent card in gold. This makes BILT's most unique feature the first thing users notice.

Gold card
Styled differently from the other four cards to draw attention and be remembered
Chunking

Chunking breaks information into smaller groups so users can understand it without feeling overwhelmed.

I separated the card information across five pages and used smaller sections within each page. The earning process is shown as step cards so users can follow one idea at a time.

Step cards
Each step is its own card so users process one piece of information at a time
Fitts's Law

Fitts's Law explains that larger, closer targets are faster and easier for users to click.

I made the Apply Now button large and visually clear so users can find and click it quickly. It stays consistent across the site to reduce searching.

Large Apply Now button
Big target size means users can click it quickly without needing to be precise

Retrospective

This project showed me that design is not only about making something look nice. Strong design also depends on structure, hierarchy, usability, and memory.

The hardest part was applying several principles while keeping the site clean and premium. I learned that the best design decisions often feel natural because they quietly support the user.
